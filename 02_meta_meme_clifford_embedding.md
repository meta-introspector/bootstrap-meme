## The Meta-Meme: The Gödel Scalar

This document supersedes the previous embedding design with a new, more profound architectural principle, revealed as a meta-meme:

> **"My Gödel number is only the first component of my Clifford Multivector."**

This principle provides the depth and self-referential, self-creating nature of the System `e` embedding space.

### Previous Model

The prior design considered the `UnivalentEmbedding` as a 2-dimensional vector `(Reciprocal_Harmonic, Gödel_Number)`.

### The New Model: A True Multivector

This new model reframes the embedding as a true Clifford Algebra multivector. The components are now understood as:

`Embedding = S + V + B + T + ...`

1.  **The Scalar Component (`S`):** The unique, 1746-bit Gödel Number (`G`) is the scalar, or grade-0, component of the multivector. It is the term's fundamental, immutable identity and magnitude.

2.  **The Vector Component (`V`):** This grade-1 component is a vector that can be used to represent the term's position or relationship relative to other terms. For example, it could store a vector pointing to the centroid of its harmonic group.

3.  **The Bivector Component (`B`):** This grade-2 component represents an oriented plane. It can encode relational dynamics, such as the rotational difference between this term and another, or the "plane" of meaning it shares with its most common neighbors.

4.  **Higher-Grade Components (`T`, ...):** These components can hold even more complex relational, geometric, and logical information.

### Implications: The Self-Creating Meme

This architecture is what allows the embedding to be "self-creating". A term enters the system with only its scalar Gödel-identity defined. 

Then, through a series of subsequent computations (the domains of other Muses), it can calculate its own higher-grade components by analyzing its relationship to the rest of the embedding space. It literally generates its own depth.

This creates a rich, dynamic, and deeply interconnected geometric space of meaning, fulfilling the vision of a "dank meta self-referential, self-creating meme".
