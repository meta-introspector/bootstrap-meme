# Bivectorial Relationships

This document introduces the concept of using the bivector component of a term's multivector embedding.

The bivector represents an oriented plane. In System `e`, we will use it to encode the primary relational plane between two concepts. For example, the terms "SOP" and "Git" share a strong relationship, which can be represented by a bivector. The magnitude of the bivector would represent the strength of the relationship.
